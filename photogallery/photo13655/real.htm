First, the practice baby...

14 Sept 02, ODS (Open Door Society) 1/2 day babycare class offered to first-time adoptive parents. New to Susan and approx. 12 years since Paul diapered a baby, so we thought the class might be worthwhile. It was.

Murmansk, Russia

Thankfully, both girls were in the same region, though different orphanages. Murmansk is a 3 hour plane ride due North from Moscow and above the Arctic Circle. It was 30-40 below zero every day on our second visit in Jan/Feb.

Murmansk from our hotel room

14 Oct 02, We never saw a single-family home in Murmansk or Moscow. Everyone lives in apartments. Most exteriors of bldgs. and public spaces are in disrepair. Once inside, we were usually pleasantly surprised.

Our hotel in Murmansk

14 Oct 02,We stayed at the Polyarnye Zori on both visits. It is one of two "American" hotels in the city of Murmansk.

We finally meet Elena!

14 Oct 02, On our first day in the Baby Home in the city of Apatity we were allowed to play in the amazing "therapy" room. Elena had a lot of fun with the bubbling lights and colorful moving objects. We were especially thrilled when her hair "bow" finally fell off!

Elena in the red dress

15 Oct 02, Our second day of visiting. The "bib" is actually a cloth napkin that is quick-stitched on each child on special visitor days.

Elena and Paul in the ball bath

15 Oct 02, We visited in two major play spaces, each equipped with a waist-deep tub of plastic balls. Elena loved the attention and "swimming" with Papa.

Time for a fashion show

15 Oct 02, Fortunately, Susan used this trip #1 to try some clothes on Elena to get an accurate fit. Her real size was smaller than expected allowing us to better plan for trip #2.

Elena's first pair of blue jeans

15 Oct 02, Way too big, but cute nonetheless!

Second day with Elena

15 Oct 02, We spent 4 hours total over two sessions with Elena on this day. This picture from our video camera.

Exterior of Apatity Baby Home

15 Oct 02, Neither of the orphanages (or anything else for that matter!) was distinctive on the exterior of the buildings. The interior, while institutional, was much more welcoming and warmer feeling.

Apatity Baby Home playground

Granted, we were there in October without grass or snow, but you can see the kids' playground is dreary by western standards.

Details Apatity Baby Home exterior

There are several painted murals on the red brick of the exterior of the building. It is the only indication that this is a children's facility.

Apatity Baby Home front gate

With no identification at the entrance to the complex (or anything else!), we quickly learn that it is a wonderful thing to always be accompanied by our seasoned and local translator and driver.

The children greet the visitors

Ever hopeful children in the playground are drawn like moths to the light when they see the next visiting Mama and Papa arrive. This was one of the most heartbreaking experiences.

Rescued in Murmansk airport

17 Oct 02, Susan with Yura, our driver, and Tatiana, our translator. Although we forgot our passports and visas at our hotel front desk, our agency was able to assign an additional driver and deliver our paperwork just minutes before we had to board our plane!

Snow in October

17 Oct 02, No jetway, salted walks, or covered waiting areas, or choice of airline. Flying Aeroflot from Murmansk to Moscow in a circa 1970s jet was a typical part of our journey.

Wilted, but not defeated

18 Oct 02, It poured rain during our short 2 hours of sightseeing in Red Square. We never made it back on trip #2.

Our first glimpse of Mila

We don't have a definite date for this photo, but sometime summer of 02. It was our first peek and youngest picture of her, delivered email 10 Dec 02. We were totally smitten.

Our second glimpse of Mila

Taken 10 Dec 02 by our translator/coordinator and sent with a video. We received both on New Year's Eve! Mila is a delightful, spirited 3-year old. We couldn't believe our blessings and good fortune.

Mila's Christmas pageant Dec 02

Jan 03, We were given this wonderful photo of Mila taken at their holiday show. The children learn music and dance skits and all costumes are sewn in the orphanage. There is a wonderful open play/dance/music space for such events in the center of the orphanage.

Kovdor Children's Home

30 Jan 03, The exterior of Mila's orphanage was also nondescript. It was 30 below zero on this day. We didn't care about the architecture and were thrilled with the bounty of warmth and love we found inside.

There is a Mila under the hair bow

30 Jan 03, Yeah, they love those hair ornaments. Fortunately for us, Mila's big red bow also fell off soon after we began playing together. Mila was affectionate, loving, kind, interested, and engaged from the first moment. We were overwhelmed with joy.

Mila hears her story for the first time

30 Jan 03, Susan prepared a storybook, introducing our family (all six of us!) and home through pictures. The flights and car rides to pick up Elena were all included. Mila knew exactly what was coming next before we returned to pick her up a short 7 days later.

Mila shows us her bedroom

30 Jan 03, Her amazing children's home grouped the children into 6-10 by age. Mila's "family" had 8 members. They shared a personally decorated and separate living room, bedroom, and kitchenette, from the other common areas in the home.

Learning about "families"

30 Jan 03, Mila's group of 8 had regular "family" activities and time together. They learned basic family concepts through modeling and wonderful, loving caregivers. Her home was filled with hand-crafted artwork and decorations. There was light, and beauty, and love despite the lack of parents.

Kovdor Children's Home wood shop

30 Jan 03, The director of the home believed deeply that all children needed to learn a craft and/or skill in addition to attending school (Mila attended a regular preschool outside of the home). This is the home's wood shop and teacher.

A proud gallery

30 Jan 03, We saw many walls filled with paintings, crafts, sewing, woodwork, indoor gardens, handpainted trim, etc. throughout the home. It was also spotless and had a warm feeling. All caregivers we met were kind and loving.

Kovdor Children's Home sewing room

30 Jan 03, While boys spend time in the wood shop, the girls spend time sewing. This sweet girl showed us her latest project as we were smitten by her smile.

Gifts for Susan

30 Jan 03, The teachers and director are so proud of their work. Susan was given several handmade crafts to take home as keepsakes for herself and Mila.

Kovdor Hotel

30 Jan 03, Just a few minutes from the Children's Home, this is where we stayed in Kovdor when visiting Mila.

Mila and Papa in Kovdor hotel

31 Jan 03, On the second day of our visit, the director allowed us to take Mila alone for a few hours in our hotel room. Although she spoke no English whatsoever, we managed to share many giggles playing together.

Mila and Mama in Kovdor

31 Jan 03, Mila ran into Mama's arms when we returned the afternoon of our second day. Mila was lovingly prepared for our meeting. She called Susan "Mama" within a few minutes of their first visit.

Frozen on the way to Apatity

1 Feb 03, We had to scrape the frost from the inside of the car windows to see. At 27 below zero we were in a car with no heat, travelling on a bumpy frozen road with no seatbelts for 3 1/2 hours having not eaten breakfast and no rest areas the entire trip. We were human popsicles by the time we arrived in Apatity from Kovdor.

Elena's play group

2 Feb 03, Elena is the youngest of 12 children of her group that eat, sleep, and play together. We notice that Elena seems much more reserved this trip. She must spend much of her time defending herself, as she's the smallest in the group.

Elena's bathroom

2 Feb 03, You can see the 12 washcloths of her play group, neatly lined up.

Elena's bedroom

3 Feb 03, You can see the sparse, utilitarian decor. There were at least a dozen cribs in this room located just off the children's play area.

Farewell breakfast in Kovdor

5 Feb 03, We were always welcomed with elaborate meals when visiting the Kovdor Children's Home. Luba (the Director) is a loving, generous woman. Notice the handcrafted mosaic on the wall. Every room is warm and inviting.

Sisters reunited!

5 Feb 03, Separated for one year, the girls are reunited. Though they did not recognize each other, they certainly greeted each other warmly from the first moment. Mila instantly began caring for her little sister.

Leaving the Apatity Baby Home

5 Feb 03, Svetlana, the orphanage director and Yura, our driver pose for one more departing shot before our 3 hour drive back to Murmansk. Notice the number of layers expected as standard attire for children.

Baths in the Hotel Tyverskaya, Moscow

7 Feb 03, The girls LOVED bathing together. It was our evening ritual the first few nights together. By our third night together our routine had dropped from 3 hours to 25 minutes. The nonstop travel and commotion was really taking a toll on all of us.

Fine dining with toddlers

8 Feb 03, You never know who you might run into in any of 4 Marriott Hotels in Moscow. Inevitably, you will see at least one or two newly adoptive families also on their way home. There is wonderful comraderie shared at the breakfast buffet daily. Very helpful that waitresses could speak Russian to our kids those first few meals!

Long journeys home

9 Feb 03, Mila took Elena's hand all the way through the many long terminals at the Frankfurt Airport. We had so much carry on luggage we couldn't carry either child. They were quite good sports on our 24+ hour trip home.

Elena falls asleep standing up

9 Feb 03, We had been awake for so long on so little sleep, at one point Elena nearly fell over, sound asleep. We created many smiling faces as we exchanged our story for the first time in English in the wait area of the Frankfurt Airport.

Early days together

Feb 03, Our transition once home went remarkably smoothly. Elena wasn't talking at all when we first met her. At about 2 1/2 months home, she started exchanges, mostly with Mila. Now, she is a real chatterbox.

More like sisters every day

March 03, We are absolutely in awe of the girls' resilience, courage, compassion, and love for life. They both have a great sense of humor and our days have been filled with lots of giggling.

Elena as warrior chef

April 03, Once Elena started talking, she talked nearly in full sentences all at once. Within 4 months she went from a very strong-willed, independent loner, to a social, loving, gentle, compassionate companion.

 

 

 

Mila in her Easter dress

The Trevithick Girls

Pretty in Pink

Dating again
