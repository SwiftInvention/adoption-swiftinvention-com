Our Adoption Journey

Click here for our chronological photo gallery from September 2002 to September 2003.

We started our adoption journey in February, 2002, and the next February we were home from Russia with Mila, age 3 1/2, and Elena, age 23 months. We had been expecting to add only one daughter to our family, between the ages of 0-18 months. During our first trip to Apatity to visit Elena, we were surprised and delighted to learn that Elena's older sister would not be placed in a permanent foster family as we had been told earlier.

Unfortunately, not long after we heard the exciting news, we were then told that Mila was not, and never would be made available for international adoption. Mila's paperwork had not been returned nor posted on the international waiting list. She was in a different city, Kovdor, (although luckily the same region of Murmansk) and in an orphanage from which no American agency had ever adopted children.

Through our perseverance and the tireless efforts of our agency and their contacts in Murmansk, we were eventually granted our wish, and a judge in Murmansk accepted our petition to also adopt Mila. Though our second trip to bring Elena home was delayed by perhaps as much as 2 months, we were allowed to bring Mila home after only one trip to Kovdor instead of the usual two, and the extra 10-day required wait period after our court date was also waived.

MAPS International (Maine Adoption Placement Services), we love you! Our beautiful daughters would never have been brought together without your expertise. We are also forever grateful for the love and care both girls were given by their respective institutions. Elena at the Baby Home in Apatity, and especially Mila at the Kovdor Children's Home.  It is a wonderful orphanage, we cannot say enough good things about it, or its Director, Luba. Both children had clearly been treated with respect and love.

To top off all our good fortune, we have just recently learned that as a result of Mila's placement in our family, another Massachusetts couple is waiting to bring home their son from the same Kovdor Children's Home, and remarkably, Mila's same play group.

A special thank you, too, to Matthew and Harlan, big brother and sister, for welcoming these little (messy, very messy) people in your lives all the while facing your own teenage challenges. We love you for the grace, generosity, and kindness you've shown through the huge transition. We are absolutely busting with pride at the remarkable young adults you have become.

Much love,

Paul and Susan Trevithick (ptrevithick@alum.mit.edu, susan@philographica.com)
